\title{Termination Checking for a dependently typed language}
\author{Karl Mehltretter}
\date{today}

\begin{abstract}
This thesis deals with the problem of termination checking for theorem provers bases on dependend types.

With the well-known \emph{Curry Howard isomorphism}, proofs and propositions can be thought of as terms and types of
functional languages. 
Proof checking then becomes type checking of functions.
Simple termination checkers rely on strucural orderings to show that funcions are terminating (i.e the proof is a real one). 
But quite a lot of proofs are rejected by these simple termination checkers.

Some authors have suggested \emph{sized types}: using data types that are annotated with a size index and to ensure termination
through the type sytem. As dependend types already offer annotations through the notion of inductive families, the question is how the concept of sized types could be integrated into a theorem prover based on depentend types.
Sized types play to their strength with higher order data types.
Types like infinitly branchings trees, which are often needed in theorem proving, can be handeled by sized types.

To this end, a prototype theorem prover loosly inspired by the theorem prover Agda2 was implemented.

As termination is to inductive functions, productivity is wantend for coinductive functions.
The implementation was extended to handle coinductive definitions.
\end{abstract}
